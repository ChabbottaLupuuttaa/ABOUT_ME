About Me

Fueled by a passion for crafting user-friendly digital experiences that empower everyone, I'm a third-year computer science student excitedly exploring the realm of web design. Obsessed with user-centered design and building beautiful, accessible systems, I'm driven to break down barriers and make the web a welcoming place for all.

Early Beginnings

Arriving prematurely at seven months, my arrival was deemed a fortunate event. The name "Chabota" means "good" in Tonga, a fitting tribute to my early entrance into the world. Growing up in vibrant, sun-soaked Livingstone, where even the sunsets blaze with optimism, instilled in me an unwavering resilience and a deep love for warm hues, both of which I now pour into my design work.

After completing primary and secondary school in Southern Province, I yearned to travel to a distant place. The Copperbelt, with its promise of new experiences and challenges, called to me. And as they say, everything works out in time.

If I could meet my fifteen-year-old self, I'd offer one piece of advice, borrowed from one of my favorite poets, Oscar Wilde: "Be yourself, because everyone else is taken." I'd also urge him to pick up some literature and discover the magic of storytelling.

Dreams & Ambitions

My dreams stretch far and wide, but one vision burns brightest: a future where technology empowers, not divides. Imagine a world where everyone, regardless of ability, can navigate the web with ease and unlock its endless possibilities. That's the world I'm striving to build, one inclusive design at a time. Through honed skills in web development, accessibility, and UX design, I'm ready to bridge the digital gap and make the web a brighter, more inclusive space for everyone.
